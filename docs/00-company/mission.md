This document defines the mission of Clada Systems and translates it into present-day execution priorities.

## Mission Statement

Clada Systems builds Clada OS, an operational software platform for contractor-led businesses, starting with SolarGRANT Pro for Irish solar installers.

The mission is to help contractor-led teams capture demand, manage work, protect trust, reduce administrative drag, and make better operational decisions.

## Why This Exists

The mission turns the company vision into a practical mandate. It should help the team decide what to build, what to ignore, what to research, and how to sequence work.

The mission also protects the company from two common failures:

- building technology because it is interesting rather than useful;
- expanding into new markets before the first market has been properly served.

## What The Mission Defines

The mission defines the work Clada Systems is responsible for now:

- Build the Clada OS platform deliberately.
- Make SolarGRANT Pro excellent for Irish solar installers.
- Treat trust, reliability, and operational clarity as product requirements.
- Use documentation as part of the production system.
- Learn from real customer workflows before extracting platform abstractions.

The mission does not require Clada Systems to solve every contractor problem immediately. It requires the company to solve real problems deeply enough that customers trust the system in daily work.

## Current Execution Mandate

The current execution mandate is narrow:

1. Focus implementation on SolarGRANT Pro.
2. Build reusable Clada OS capabilities only when reuse is justified by real workflow evidence.
3. Keep the product practical enough for contractor-led businesses with limited time, mixed technical maturity, and operational pressure.
4. Maintain documentation, auditability, and engineering standards as part of delivery.
5. Avoid positioning SolarGRANT Pro as the entire platform.

## How This Influences Clada

The mission influences company decisions by ranking the work:

1. Customer reality before internal preference.
2. Product usefulness before technical novelty.
3. Trust before speed where customer data, compliance, or business-critical decisions are involved.
4. Focus before expansion.
5. Reusable systems before repeated one-off work, when reuse is justified.

For example, an automated document review feature should be judged by whether it makes installer operations more reliable and reviewable, not by whether it sounds technically advanced. A new market idea should be judged by what it teaches Clada OS, not by the appeal of a larger addressable market.

## Decision Rules

Use the mission to make decisions as follows:

- If a proposal does not improve a real contractor workflow, do not build it.
- If a proposal makes SolarGRANT Pro harder to understand, support, or trust, narrow it.
- If a proposal introduces platform abstraction without evidence, defer it or keep it module-local.
- If a proposal affects customer commitments, compliance, or automation boundaries, document it before implementation.
- If a proposal is useful but distracts from solar product-market fit, record it for future strategy rather than implementing it now.

## Mission Metrics

The mission should be measured through operational outcomes, not vanity metrics.

Useful signals include:

- faster lead response and qualification;
- fewer missed follow-ups;
- cleaner document collection and review;
- lower administrative burden for installer teams;
- clearer customer communication;
- higher confidence in grant-related workflows;
- reduced need for duplicate spreadsheets, email chasing, and manual status tracking;
- increased customer willingness to rely on Clada OS as a daily operating system.
