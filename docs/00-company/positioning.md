This document defines how Clada Systems should be described to customers, future employees, advisors, investors, and contributors. It protects clear language and prevents the company from being positioned as a generic software tool, a single solar grant product, or an AI-first novelty.

## Core Positioning

Clada Systems is a professional Irish software company building operational software for contractor-led businesses.

Clada OS is the platform.

SolarGRANT Pro is the first product module on Clada OS, focused on Irish solar installers and their grant-related operational workflows.

## Why This Exists

Positioning shapes product decisions. If the company is described poorly, the product will eventually be built poorly.

This document exists to keep language calm, confident, practical, and consistent with the [Constitution](../CONSTITUTION.md). It should guide website copy, product documents, investor explanations, sales conversations, repository documentation, and AI-generated text.

## What It Defines

Positioning defines:

- the category Clada Systems belongs in;
- the distinction between company, platform, and product module;
- the tone the company should use;
- the claims the company should avoid;
- the customer problems Clada Systems should emphasise.

## Positioning Hierarchy

Use this hierarchy consistently:

1. Clada Systems: the company.
2. Clada OS: the operational software platform.
3. SolarGRANT Pro: the first product module.
4. Irish solar grant workflows: the first implementation focus.

Do not describe SolarGRANT Pro as the company. Do not describe solar grant assistance as the full platform.

## Plain-Language Explanation

Clada Systems builds operational software for contractor-led businesses.

Its platform, Clada OS, helps these businesses manage the work that sits between sales, administration, documents, customer communication, compliance, and delivery.

The first product module, SolarGRANT Pro, helps Irish solar installers manage lead qualification, grant-related documentation, customer follow-up, and installer review workflows.

## Tone

Clada Systems should sound:

- calm;
- professional;
- specific;
- operationally serious;
- grounded in Irish business reality;
- confident without exaggeration.

The company should avoid:

- startup hype;
- inflated claims;
- generic SaaS phrasing;
- AI marketing language;
- language that implies full automation where human review is required;
- language that treats every contractor-led industry as already supported.

## Recommended Language

Use language like:

- "operational software for contractor-led businesses";
- "Clada OS is the platform";
- "SolarGRANT Pro is the first product module";
- "built first for Irish solar installers";
- "designed around lead qualification, documents, review, and customer communication";
- "trust, auditability, and workflow clarity are product requirements".

Avoid language like:

- "all-in-one AI platform";
- "revolutionary SaaS";
- "fully automated grant engine";
- "the platform for every contractor industry" before expansion is real;
- "SolarGRANT Pro is Clada OS";
- "just a CRM".

## Differentiation

Clada Systems should differentiate through operating depth, not marketing claims.

Important points of difference:

- The product is built around contractor-led workflows rather than generic pipeline management.
- Trust is treated as a product feature through clear status, review, auditability, and privacy-aware design.
- Documentation First Development gives the company a durable operating system for decisions.
- SolarGRANT Pro gives the platform a real market laboratory before expansion.
- Clada OS capabilities are intended to become reusable only when customer evidence supports reuse.

## How This Influences Decisions

Positioning should be used when:

- naming product concepts;
- writing user-facing copy;
- preparing sales or investor material;
- deciding whether to highlight AI or automation;
- assessing whether a feature belongs at platform or module level;
- reviewing documentation for terminology drift.

If a sentence makes the product sound broader, more automated, or more mature than it is, rewrite it.
