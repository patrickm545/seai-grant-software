This document defines the company strategy for Clada Systems during the current foundation period. It explains where the company is focused, why that focus matters, and how future expansion should be evaluated.

## Strategic Thesis

Contractor-led businesses need operational software that understands how leads, customers, jobs, documents, compliance, field work, and administration connect.

Many of these businesses are too operationally complex for simple spreadsheets, but not well served by generic enterprise software. Clada Systems exists to build practical operating software for this middle ground.

The strategic entry point is Irish solar. The platform destination is Clada OS. The first product module is SolarGRANT Pro.

## Why This Exists

Strategy protects focus. Clada Systems has a broad long-term opportunity, but broad opportunity can create weak execution if it is pursued too early.

This document defines the current sequence:

1. Build and learn in Irish solar.
2. Reach product-market fit with SolarGRANT Pro.
3. Extract proven reusable capabilities into Clada OS.
4. Expand into adjacent contractor-led markets only when the platform and company are ready.

## Current Strategic Focus

Solar remains the sole implementation focus until product-market fit has been achieved.

This means:

- new product work should serve SolarGRANT Pro unless explicitly approved as platform foundation;
- future industries may influence naming, architecture, and strategic planning;
- no future-industry workflow should be implemented before the solar module has earned sustained customer trust;
- reusable Clada OS capability work should be justified by evidence from the solar implementation or a clear platform risk.

## Strategic Pillars

### 1. Win One Market With Operational Depth

Clada Systems should win trust with Irish solar installers by solving their real operational problems better than their current patchwork of tools.

Depth means the product should understand lead intake, grant eligibility, customer communication, document preparation, installer review, and operational status. It should not stop at surface-level CRM fields.

### 2. Build The Platform Through The Module

SolarGRANT Pro should teach Clada OS what deserves to become reusable.

Examples of likely platform capabilities include:

- intake and qualification workflows;
- customer and business records;
- document collection and review;
- workflow status tracking;
- audit trails;
- role-based operational views;
- human-reviewed automation;
- communication history.

These should become platform-level only when their boundaries are clear enough to serve more than one workflow.

### 3. Make Trust A Differentiator

Contractor-led businesses rely on reputation. Software that creates confusion, hides decisions, loses data, or overpromises automation will not be trusted.

Clada Systems should use reliability, auditability, privacy, clear status, review points, and professional communication as strategic advantages.

### 4. Keep The Product Practical

The product should feel like dependable operational software, not a technology showcase.

This affects:

- interface density and clarity;
- naming;
- copy;
- workflow sequencing;
- permissions and review;
- reporting;
- customer onboarding.

### 5. Maintain Documentation As Strategy

Documentation First Development is not only an engineering habit. It is a strategic operating model.

High-quality documentation lets Clada Systems:

- onboard future employees and AI coding agents;
- preserve decisions across milestones;
- avoid repeating strategic debates;
- explain the platform to advisors and investors;
- keep product, design, engineering, and leadership aligned.

## Expansion Logic

Future industries should be evaluated through evidence, not excitement.

A future market becomes a serious candidate only when:

1. the workflow resembles proven Clada OS capabilities;
2. the buyer has a clear operational pain and budget;
3. the company can explain why existing tools are insufficient;
4. expansion would strengthen the platform rather than fragment it;
5. the team can support the market without weakening SolarGRANT Pro.

Until then, future markets should remain strategy inputs, not implementation scope.

## Strategic Trade-Offs

Clada Systems should accept these trade-offs deliberately:

- It is better to be trusted in one market than vaguely relevant in many.
- It is better to ship a narrower workflow that is reliable than a broad workflow customers cannot depend on.
- It is better to document a decision before building than to move quickly into unclear architecture.
- It is better to keep automation human-reviewed where risk is high than to promise full autonomy prematurely.
- It is better to delay abstraction than to build a platform model unsupported by customer evidence.

## Decision Use

Use this strategy when deciding:

- whether a feature belongs in SolarGRANT Pro or Clada OS;
- whether to research a future industry;
- whether to accept a customer request that widens scope;
- whether to invest in platform infrastructure;
- whether to change positioning, pricing, onboarding, or partnership direction.

Any decision that changes the platform strategy, target market, or product-module boundary should link to this document and may require an ADR.
