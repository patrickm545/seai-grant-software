This document defines the customer segments Clada Systems serves now, the users SolarGRANT Pro should prioritise, and the future customer types Clada OS may serve after product-market fit.

## Why This Exists

Target customer clarity protects product focus. Clada Systems should not build for a vague contractor market while trying to serve a specific solar workflow.

This document helps product managers, designers, engineers, AI coding agents, advisors, and future executives understand who the company is serving, what pain they feel, how they buy, and what success means.

## Primary Customer

The primary customer is an Irish solar installation business that sells, qualifies, and coordinates residential or small commercial solar work while managing SEAI grant-related workflows.

The buyer is usually an owner, managing director, operations lead, or commercial lead who feels operational pain directly and can approve software that improves sales follow-up, administration, and customer trust.

Primary customers commonly have:

- a small to mid-sized team;
- a mix of sales, administration, survey, installation, and management roles;
- lead flow from websites, referrals, partners, paid channels, or local reputation;
- document and grant-related workflows that create administrative pressure;
- limited appetite for heavyweight enterprise software;
- a need for professional customer communication.

## Secondary Customer

The secondary customer is the homeowner or end customer served through SolarGRANT Pro workflows.

Homeowners may not buy Clada OS directly, but their experience matters. Clear communication, confidence in grant steps, document requests, and status visibility all influence installer reputation and product value.

Secondary customer needs include:

- simple explanation of next steps;
- confidence that documents are handled carefully;
- clarity on what is required from them;
- timely follow-up;
- professional communication from the installer.

## Primary Users

Within the installer business, the product should support several user groups.

### Owner Or Managing Director

Responsibilities:

- revenue;
- reputation;
- operational control;
- team performance;
- supplier and customer confidence.

Needs:

- visibility into pipeline and bottlenecks;
- confidence that leads are followed up;
- operational reporting;
- assurance that customer records and documents are handled professionally.

### Operations Manager

Responsibilities:

- workflow coordination;
- handoffs between sales, admin, survey, and installation;
- process quality;
- exception handling.

Needs:

- clear status tracking;
- fewer manual follow-ups;
- reliable task ownership;
- visibility into blocked work.

### Sales Or Lead Coordinator

Responsibilities:

- responding to leads;
- qualifying customers;
- arranging next steps;
- maintaining context.

Needs:

- fast lead intake;
- structured qualification;
- easy communication history;
- clear next actions;
- fewer duplicate records.

### Grants Or Administration Coordinator

Responsibilities:

- document requests;
- grant-related checks;
- customer reminders;
- submission preparation support;
- record accuracy.

Needs:

- document status visibility;
- review workflows;
- templates and checklists;
- audit trail;
- reduced email chasing.

### Installer, Surveyor, Or Field Lead

Responsibilities:

- site information;
- job context;
- installation readiness;
- customer handoff quality.

Needs:

- concise operational context;
- accurate customer and site information;
- visibility into missing items;
- low-friction updates.

## Business Size

SolarGRANT Pro should prioritise installer businesses large enough to feel coordination pain but small enough that operational improvement is visible quickly.

Typical early customer profile:

- founder-led or management-led;
- roughly 3 to 50 staff or contractors;
- active lead generation;
- growing sales pipeline;
- limited internal software team;
- current reliance on spreadsheets, email, messaging apps, generic CRM tools, or manual checklists.

This is not a strict eligibility rule. It is a design centre.

## Operational Maturity

Clada Systems should expect mixed maturity:

- Some customers will have no formal CRM.
- Some will have a CRM but still manage documents and grant workflows manually.
- Some will have strong sales discipline but weak administrative visibility.
- Some will have good technical staff but inconsistent process adoption.

The product should not assume a perfectly structured organisation. It should help the business become more structured without forcing a heavy process before value is visible.

## Common Operational Pain Points

Primary pain points include:

- leads not followed up quickly enough;
- customer details scattered across inboxes, spreadsheets, and messaging tools;
- uncertainty about grant eligibility or required documents;
- repeated manual customer reminders;
- unclear handoffs between sales, admin, survey, and installation;
- weak visibility into blocked applications or incomplete records;
- staff relying on personal memory rather than shared operating data;
- inconsistent customer communication;
- poor auditability when something goes wrong;
- difficulty scaling without hiring more administration capacity.

## Decision-Making Process

Purchase decisions are likely to be practical and trust-based.

The buyer will usually ask:

1. Will this save time or recover lost revenue?
2. Will the team actually use it?
3. Will it fit the way our business works?
4. Will it improve customer confidence?
5. Is our data safe?
6. Is the product clear enough to adopt without months of setup?
7. Does the company understand Irish solar operations?

The sales and onboarding experience should answer these questions directly.

## Success Measures

Customer success should be measured through operational outcomes:

- higher lead response consistency;
- shorter time from lead to qualified next step;
- fewer missed or duplicated follow-ups;
- cleaner document collection;
- clearer grant-related status;
- reduced admin time per customer;
- stronger manager visibility into pipeline and blockers;
- improved homeowner communication;
- sustained weekly use by the installer team.

## How Future Decisions Should Use This

Use this customer model when prioritising features, writing research plans, designing onboarding, reviewing product copy, and deciding whether a requested workflow belongs in SolarGRANT Pro.

Future decisions should ask:

1. Which primary user is this for?
2. Which operational pain does it reduce?
3. Does it help the buyer trust the product enough to adopt it?
4. Does it improve the homeowner experience without making installer work heavier?
5. Is this needed for Irish solar product-market fit, or is it a future expansion idea?

If a proposal cannot name the customer, pain point, and success measure, it should not move into implementation.

## Future Expansion Customers

Future Clada OS customers may include contractor-led businesses in electrical, HVAC, plumbing, roofing, construction, landscaping, retrofit, renewable energy, and related services.

These future customers should influence platform thinking but not current implementation priority.

Before expanding, Clada Systems should confirm:

- the workflow shares meaningful patterns with proven Clada OS capabilities;
- the buyer has urgent operational pain;
- the business can adopt software without heavy custom delivery;
- the expansion strengthens the platform rather than creating a one-off module;
- SolarGRANT Pro product-market fit is not compromised.
