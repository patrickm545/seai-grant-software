This document defines the long-term destination for Clada Systems. It explains what the company is trying to make true over many years and how that ambition should influence present-day decisions.

## Vision Statement

Clada Systems exists to help contractor-led businesses run with the operational clarity, trust, and control of much larger organisations.

Clada OS should become the business operating layer for contractor-led companies that manage leads, customers, jobs, documents, staff, compliance, reporting, and repeatable operational decisions.

The first proof point is SolarGRANT Pro, the first product module on Clada OS. SolarGRANT Pro focuses on Irish solar installers and their grant-related sales, administration, and customer communication workflows.

## Why This Exists

The vision prevents Clada Systems from mistaking its first workflow for its full purpose. The Irish solar market gives the company a disciplined starting point, but the durable company idea is broader: contractor-led businesses need operational software that reflects how work actually happens.

This document gives future employees, engineers, product managers, advisors, executives, and AI coding agents a stable answer to a simple question: what are we building towards?

## What The Vision Defines

This vision defines four durable ideas:

1. Clada Systems is the company.
2. Clada OS is the platform.
3. SolarGRANT Pro is the first product module.
4. Contractor-led operations are the enduring customer domain.

The vision does not approve every possible contractor workflow. It sets the direction for evaluating them.

## Long-Term Ambition

Clada OS should help contractor-led businesses understand what needs attention, what has been promised, what is blocked, what requires human review, and what should happen next.

Over time, Clada OS may support multiple contractor-led industries, including solar, electrical, HVAC, plumbing, roofing, retrofit, construction, landscaping, renewable energy, and adjacent field-service markets. Expansion should happen only when Clada Systems has learned enough from real customers to reuse platform capabilities responsibly.

The ambition is not to produce a broad generic tool. The ambition is to build a practical operating platform that can be configured into focused product modules.

## Current Horizon

Until product-market fit has been achieved, solar remains the sole implementation focus.

Future markets may be considered during strategy, architecture, naming, and data model decisions. They should not distract the team from making SolarGRANT Pro excellent for Irish solar installers.

The correct sequence is:

1. Win trust in one real market.
2. Learn which workflows repeat.
3. Extract reusable Clada OS capabilities.
4. Expand only when the platform has earned the right to do so.

## How This Influences Clada

The vision should influence planning in practical ways:

- Product teams should avoid features that make SolarGRANT Pro impressive in a demo but weak in daily operations.
- Engineers should avoid hard-coding solar-specific assumptions into platform-level code unless the trade-off is documented.
- Designers should favour clarity, speed, and trust over visual novelty.
- AI coding agents should preserve the company, platform, and module distinction in every documentation and implementation change.
- Leadership should measure progress by operational adoption, customer trust, and maintainability, not just feature count.

## Decision Tests

Use this vision when deciding whether to proceed with a product, engineering, market, or partnership decision.

Ask:

1. Does this make contractor-led businesses easier to run?
2. Does this strengthen Clada OS as a platform or SolarGRANT Pro as the first module?
3. Does this preserve trust, simplicity, and operational clarity?
4. Does this help the solar implementation now, or is it speculative expansion?
5. If this creates future platform value, is that value grounded in evidence from real workflows?

If the answer is unclear, narrow the work, gather evidence, or document the trade-off in an ADR.

## What The Vision Rules Out

Clada Systems should not become:

- a generic CRM with contractor language added afterwards;
- a marketing-led AI product where automation is the story instead of the workflow;
- a marketplace that competes with customers for homeowner attention;
- a collection of one-off tools with no shared platform discipline;
- a software consultancy disguised as a product company.

These boundaries protect focus.
