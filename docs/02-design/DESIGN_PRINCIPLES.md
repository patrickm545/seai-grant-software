These principles guide Clada OS interface design.

## Operational Clarity

Users should be able to tell what happened, what needs attention, and what action comes next. Dashboards and workflow views should prioritise status, exceptions, and next actions over decorative presentation.

## Professional Trust

The interface should feel appropriate for customer data, grants, documents, and business workflows. Use clear labels, predictable interactions, and restrained visual hierarchy.

## Human Review

When AI or automation assists a workflow, the interface must show enough context for a human to review the result. Do not hide material uncertainty behind confident language.

## Module-Aware Language

Use Clada OS for platform concepts and SolarGRANT Pro for the solar grant module. Keep customer-facing solar language where it helps module users, but do not let module terminology leak into platform architecture.

## Accessibility And Durability

Design for real work conditions: varied screen sizes, repeated use, clear contrast, readable text, and reliable form behaviour. A workflow that looks polished but slows down daily work is not finished.
